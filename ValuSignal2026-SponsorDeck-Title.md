**OBJECTION:** "It's too expensive."
**RESPONSE:** Run the cost-per-contact math. $10,000 ÷ 2,000 attendees = $5 per contact. With co-branding, a keynote, lead capture, meeting scheduling, and a full analytics package included, what does it cost you to build those individual channels to the valuation profession from scratch? The price isn't what you're paying for the event. It's what you're paying for the position.
**REDIRECT:** What's your current fully-loaded cost to generate one qualified conversation with a valuation professional? If it's less than $5 — I want to know what you're doing. If it's more — and it almost always is — the math here is already better.

**RESPONSE:** Track record is a proxy for demand signal. Here's your demand signal: three national valuation associations are co-promoting this event to their member bases. **REDIRECT:** If I could show you a signed letter of endorsement from Appraisers on Purpose, Texas Residential Valuation Society, and American Valuation Society — would that address the track record concern? Because those are the three organizations already behind this event.

## TALK TRACK — Title Tier Deck
*For use by: Eunice Lopez / Hansel Dobbs*

**OPENING (Slides 1–2):**
Open by reframing what Title is before you explain what it includes. It's not sponsorship — it's strategic alignment. The company that is the Title Sponsor of the first national valuation conference owns a position that no future investment can replicate. Establish this distinction in the first 90 seconds. The rest of the deck is justification for a decision the prospect should be making emotionally, not rationally.

**CONTEXT SETTING (Slides 3–7):**
Walk through each major asset — co-branding, keynote, homepage placement, premium booth — and show the mechanic for each. The prospect needs to visualize their brand in each placement. The Platform Dominance Map slide is the one to dwell on — it shows the totality of the investment in one visual. Ask: "Is there a moment in this event where you wouldn't want to be visible?"

**THE OFFER (Slides 8–12):**
The Data Manifest and Pipeline Creation slides are for the rational justification — the numbers the prospect will take back to their team. The Pioneer ROI Case is for the emotional close — the compounding value argument that no spreadsheet can capture. Use both. End with the four-pillar summary: Platform Dominance + Thought Leadership + Data + Direct Pipeline. Let that be the last frame before objections.

**HANDLING SILENCE / OBJECTIONS:**
Silence after the price is almost always one of two things: internal approval process or genuine uncertainty about the event's viability. Don't fill the silence — ask which one it is. "Is the question about the price, or about confidence in the event?" Those are two different conversations and they require different responses. For internal approval, offer the executive summary doc immediately. For event viability, go back to the pioneer positioning and the association endorsements.

**THE CLOSE (Final slides):**
"There is one Title Sponsor of ValuSignal 2026. That's not a sales tactic — it's the structure of the offer. I'm having this conversation because I believe your company is the right fit for this position. If you agree, the next step is a one-page agreement and we go live immediately. If you need something specific before you can make that call — tell me what it is and I'll get it to you today. What do you need?"
