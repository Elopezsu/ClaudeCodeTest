## Session Proposal

### What the AMC Actually Needs: A QC Reality Check from the Other Side of the Order

**Speaker**
**Jessie Elizabeth Ruckel, SRA, CML**
President, iappraisal | Austin, TX

Credentialed residential appraiser and appraisal management executive with 30+ years spanning independent practice, bank-side review, and full-scale AMC leadership. As President of iappraisal, Jessie oversees valuation services for lenders, AMCs, and builder partners across Texas and beyond — with a front-row seat to what separates appraisers who create friction from those who build lasting, high-volume relationships.

---

### Purpose

Appraisers hear "the AMC just wants a number." This session dismantles that myth. Jessie brings the AMC and lender side of the table into the room — pulling back the curtain on how orders are managed, how appraisers are evaluated, and what actually drives the decisions that affect your workload, your callbacks, and your reputation.

This is not a session about rules or compliance checklists. It's an honest, experience-based look at what the people managing your orders are actually thinking — and what you can do to make their job easier in ways that directly benefit yours.

---

### What This Session Covers

- How AMCs and lenders evaluate appraisers beyond turnaround time — and what signals reliability at scale
- The most common report issues that trigger callbacks before a file ever reaches underwriting — and how to eliminate them
- What communication habits distinguish appraisers AMCs want to call first from those they call last
- How lender expectations have shifted in the UAD 3.6 era and what that means for the working relationship between appraisers and AMCs
- What it takes to move from being a vendor on a panel to being a trusted, preferred partner for high-volume clients

---

### Why This Session Matters

- Most appraisers only see one side of the transaction — this session closes the gap by showing what AMCs and lenders experience on their end of every order
- Callbacks and revision requests are not random — understanding the patterns that generate them is the fastest way to reduce them
- The appraiser-AMC relationship is often misunderstood or adversarial; this session reframes it as a business partnership with clear rules that appraisers can learn and use
- In a market where volume is competitive and panels are crowded, the appraisers who understand what clients actually need are the ones who stay busy
- The UAD 3.6 transition is changing what "a clean report" means to reviewers and lenders — appraisers who adapt early will have a measurable advantage

---

### Who This Is For

- Appraisers doing lender work who want fewer revision requests and stronger client relationships
- Appraisers considering or expanding AMC relationships
- Firm owners managing client relationships and appraiser panels at scale

---

### Core Takeaway

Attendees leave with a clear, practical understanding of how AMCs and lenders evaluate appraisers and manage quality — and with specific, actionable ways to reduce friction, avoid common pitfalls, and build the kind of reputation that generates consistent, repeat work.
