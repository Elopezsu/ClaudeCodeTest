**Method:** same as the Hansel run — word-boundary brand/role matching, solo appraisal practices kept separate as audience, deduped to ≤2 contacts/company (≤3 for event platforms), each row carries personalized soft copy.

## How your network differs from Hansel's

1. **Event-platform / martech contacts** (webinar.net, Zoom, ON24, Cvent, HubSpot, Demandbase) — both sponsor prospects *and* operational vendor relationships for running the event.
2. **A large LATAM / BPO / enterprise-tech professional network** (Globant, Softtek, Twilio support, Foundever, etc.) — mostly individual contributors, **not** sponsor decision-makers; excluded from the master.

## Recommended sequence
1. **Tier A warm sponsors first** (proptech + AMC) — highest intent, you have the relationship.
2. **webinar.net + Zoom Events** — dual angle: sponsor pitch *and* "let's talk platform." High leverage given you're already evaluating Zoom Events for the event.
3. **Endorsers (GSE/Foundation)** — soft endorse/share asks, builds credibility ("Fannie in the seats").
4. **HW Media / Clayton Collins** — cross-promo (coordinate with Hansel; he has this contact too).
5. Academia + audience — batch/automated, lower priority.

## Caveats (honest)
- **Email coverage is thin:** only ~703 of 9,434 connections exported an email — most outreach will be LinkedIn DM.
- **Academia (160)** is the noisiest bucket — it caught your own alumni network; filter to actual career-services/professor contacts before any send.
- **Martech tier (4)** is speculative — those companies sponsor centrally, so an individual contact is a warm intro at best.
- **Overlap with Hansel (20 companies):** loop Hansel in before messaging so you present one face to a logo, not two.
