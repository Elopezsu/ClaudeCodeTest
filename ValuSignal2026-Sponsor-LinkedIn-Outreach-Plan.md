## PART 1 — LINKEDIN OUTREACH STRATEGY

### Core principle
You're not selling a sponsorship. You're inviting a peer into the first virtual conference built specifically for U.S. valuation professionals. That framing reduces friction and opens doors that a "sponsor us" cold pitch closes.

### Who to target at each company
| Company size | Target title |
|---|---|
| Startup / founder-led (Tier B) | Founder, CEO, Co-Founder |
| Mid-market | VP Marketing, Director of Marketing, Head of Partnerships |
| Enterprise (Tier A) | Director of Sponsorships, VP Brand, Head of Industry Relations, CMO |
| Education orgs (Tier C) | VP Partnerships, Director of Corporate Relations, Marketing Director |

### Connection request message (≤300 chars — no note on mobile, use this on desktop)
> Hi [Name] — I run events for ValuSignal, the first virtual conference for U.S. appraisal professionals. Given what [Company] is building, thought it made sense to connect.

**Rules:**
- Never ask for a call in the connection request
- Personalize with 1 specific thing about their company or recent post
- Do not use "I wanted to reach out" — it signals cold pitch immediately

### Follow-up message (send 2–3 days after connection accepts)
**Version A — Tier B tech/AI companies:**
> [Name], congrats on what [Company] is doing in [specific area — e.g., "automated adjustments" / "photo-based condition scoring"]. We're building ValuSignal 2026, a virtual conference for 2,000–3,000 appraisers, AMC leaders, and valuation tech buyers across all 50 states. Given your audience overlap, I wanted to share our sponsor brief — would that be useful?

**Version B — Tier A large sponsors:**
> [Name] — we're launching ValuSignal 2026, the first virtual conference built specifically for the U.S. appraisal and valuation profession. ~2,000–3,000 attendees: appraisers, firm owners, lending/fintech decision-makers. One Title Sponsor slot available ($10K, exclusive). Wanted to put it in front of [Company] before we go wider. Worth a 20-min call?

**Version C — Tier C education/community:**
> [Name] — ValuSignal 2026 is building the first national virtual hub for appraisers and valuation educators. Given [Company]'s reach in CE/licensing, a sponsorship could get [Company] in front of every serious appraiser we attract. Happy to send the deck — would that help?

### Cadence
```
Day 0   → Send connection request (personalized note)
Day 2-3 → Connection accepted → Send follow-up message (Version A/B/C)
Day 6-7 → No reply → One soft bump: "Wanted to make sure this didn't get buried — happy to send the deck if useful."
Day 14  → No reply → Move to email (eunice@valusignal.com) with subject: "ValuSignal 2026 — [Company] Sponsor Opportunity"
Day 21  → No reply → Archive. Revisit in Week 3 if momentum from early closes creates FOMO.
```

### What NOT to do
- Don't send the full deck unsolicited in message 1
- Don't pitch price in the first message
- Don't use InMail for Tier B founders — most have open profiles; regular connection + message converts better
- Don't connect with more than 15–20 people per day (LinkedIn flags bulk behavior)
