## OBJECTIONS BANK — Featured Partner / $2,500

**OBJECTION:** "It's too expensive for a new event."
**RESPONSE:** Pioneer pricing exists because you're taking pioneer risk. In Year 2, if ValuSignal grows to 3,000+ attendees and the event has a track record, a booth, lead list, speaking slot, and dedicated email won't be available for $2,500. The price reflects where the event is right now — the value is priced for where it's going.
**REDIRECT:** What would this package cost you if you tried to build it yourself — an email to 2,000 warm valuation professionals, a speaking slot, and a lead list? That number is the comparison, not a general marketing budget line.

---

**OBJECTION:** "We don't have resources to staff a virtual booth during the event."
**RESPONSE:** You don't need to staff it to benefit from it. The booth runs passively — video plays, content downloads, attendees visit. Lead capture is automatic whether or not a rep is online. Staffing with live reps amplifies the return, but it's not a requirement. You can decide how active you want to be on event day.
**REDIRECT:** How many reps could realistically be available for a few hours on event day? Even one rep handling live chats for a 3-hour block produces conversations. We can work around your team's capacity.

---

**OBJECTION:** "We need to see the full attendee list or demographics before committing."
**RESPONSE:** The event is in September 2026 — the final attendee list doesn't exist yet. What I can share is our projection methodology: we're promoting through three national valuation associations to their member bases, direct campaigns to practicing appraisers, and content marketing in the profession. The audience is 100% valuation ecosystem — that's the guarantee, not a specific headcount.
**REDIRECT:** What's the minimum list size that would make the math work for you at $2,500? Let's see if our projected range covers it.

---

**OBJECTION:** "What's the guaranteed ROI?"
**RESPONSE:** No responsible event can guarantee ROI — anyone who does is telling you what you want to hear. What's measurable and guaranteed: your booth visit count, your lead list size, your email open rate, your speaking slot attendance. Whether those inputs convert depends on your follow-up. The inputs are controlled. The output is yours to drive.
**REDIRECT:** At your average deal size in this space, how many of the 2,000 attendees would you need to convert to one deal? That's the break-even number. Does the Featured package give you enough surface area to hit that?

## TALK TRACK — Featured Tier Deck
*For use by: Eunice Lopez / Hansel Dobbs*

**OPENING (Slides 1–2):**
Open with the distinction that matters: Featured is not Core with extras — it's a pipeline tool. The prospect considering Featured is asking a different question than the Core buyer: not "will people see my brand?" but "will I generate leads?" Establish that distinction in the first 60 seconds.

**CONTEXT SETTING (Slides 3–5):**
Walk through each tool with its mechanism. Don't just list benefits — explain what each one actually does. The booth captures leads automatically. The lead list is warm contacts. The email arrives with event credibility. Each mechanic removes a risk the prospect is silently holding.

**THE OFFER (Slides 6–9):**
The ROI slide is the pivot point. Run the math live if you can — ask the prospect their average deal size, then calculate break-even on the spot. "If your average client is worth more than $2,500, you need one conversion from 2,000 warm contacts. Does that feel achievable?" Let them answer.

**HANDLING SILENCE / OBJECTIONS:**
The most common Featured objection is "it's a new event." Don't fight it — flip it. "Pioneer pricing exists because you're taking pioneer risk. Year 2, this package is more expensive and the founding positioning is gone." Silence after the price slide usually means internal approval is the real issue. Offer the one-pager formatted for internal use.

**THE CLOSE (Final slides):**
"Ten Featured slots. I'm having this conversation with multiple companies. The first 10 to sign get founding positioning and priority on speaking slot scheduling. What's the next step on your end — should I send the agreement, or is there something you need before we do that?"
