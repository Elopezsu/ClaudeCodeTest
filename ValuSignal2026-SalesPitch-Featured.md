## OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

**"$2,500 is too much for an unproven event."**
> "In Year 2, a booth, an attendee list, a speaking slot, and a dedicated email to 300–600 warm valuation professionals won't be available for $2,500. You're getting founding pricing because the event is new. The risk is real, but so is the upside — and if attendance falls below 500, the agreement includes a partial refund clause."

**"What if the lead list is small?"**
> "Our projection is 300–600 based on three national association partners. The lead list is every registered attendee — it's a real, auditable number. And you get the total attendance count in writing within 5 business days of the event."

**"We don't need a speaking slot."**
> "You don't have to use it. But it's included — and five minutes in front of a focused audience of appraisers, lenders, and valuation tech buyers is worth keeping on the table. You can pre-record it if live is a barrier."

**"How does the booth work exactly?"**
> "You get a branded page in the Zoom Events Expo Hall. Attendees navigate to it from the event lobby. They can watch your company video, download your PDFs, and chat live with your rep. Every single person who visits is automatically logged — name, email, organization — without them having to fill out a form."

**"Can we sync leads directly to our CRM?"**
> "The leads come as a CSV export post-event, delivered within 5 business days. If you want it feeding directly into your CRM, that's doable via Zapier — your team would set that up on your side."

**"We need to check with our marketing team."**
> "Totally fine. I'll send the agreement. There are 10 Featured slots and we're actively in conversations — I'd rather you have the document in hand while you're deciding."
