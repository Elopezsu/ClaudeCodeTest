## TALK TRACK — Core Tier Deck
*For use by: Eunice Lopez / Hansel Dobbs*

**OPENING (Slides 1–2):**
Open by naming exactly what this is: the entry-level sponsorship, $1,000, brand presence. Don't oversell it as something it's not — Core is visibility, not pipeline. Set that expectation immediately so the conversation is with the right buyer.

**CONTEXT SETTING (Slides 3–5):**
Walk through where the brand actually appears — be specific. Website, emails, Expo Hall, social. The prospect needs to visualize their logo in those places. The Expo Hall slide is where you show the attendee's screen — make it visual, not abstract.

**THE OFFER (Slides 6–7):**
Lead with the Pioneer angle first, then the ROI math. The founding sponsor story is the emotional close; the $0.50/impression math is the rational justification. Use both. The "What sponsors get wrong" slide is permission to reframe how they think about reach — use it to differentiate ValuSignal's audience quality from generic ad spend.

**HANDLING SILENCE / OBJECTIONS:**
If they go quiet after pricing, ask: "Is the question about budget, or about whether logo visibility alone matches what you're trying to accomplish?" That splits the objection — budget is a Featured conversation, visibility goals are a Core conversation.

**THE CLOSE (Final slides):**
"20 Core slots, first come first confirmed. The process is a one-page agreement and a logo file — Eunice handles onboarding personally, usually live on the site within a week. What's the best next step for you — should I send the agreement today, or do you need a specific piece of information first?"
