## OBJECTIONS BANK — Master Deck

**OBJECTION:** "This is a new event — how do I know anyone will actually show up?"
**RESPONSE:** That's exactly the right question, and here's the honest answer: you don't have a prior year's attendance number to point to because this event has never existed before. What you do have is the first national virtual conference built exclusively for U.S. appraisers, backed by Appraisers on Purpose, Texas Residential Valuation Society, and American Valuation Society — organizations whose members are our audience. The industry has been waiting for this platform.
**REDIRECT:** The companies that moved early on the first Inman, first Finovate didn't have attendance data either. They had a read on the market. What's your read on whether the valuation profession needs this?

**OBJECTION:** "We've sponsored virtual events before and didn't get much from it."
**RESPONSE:** That experience is common, and it's almost always a platform problem — logo on a website, maybe a banner in a Zoom webinar. ValuSignal runs on Zoom Events with a full Expo Hall: your booth has video, live rep chat, content downloads, and lead capture. You know exactly who visited and for how long. If you didn't get ROI from a previous virtual event, it's because the infrastructure wasn't there to capture it.
**REDIRECT:** Walk me through what that previous sponsorship looked like — was there a booth with lead capture, or was it more of a logo placement? If logo only — that's Core-level exposure. Featured gives you the tools that actually produce pipeline.

---

**OBJECTION:** "I need to check with my team / get budget approval."
**RESPONSE:** Completely understood. What I want to make sure of is that you have everything you need to make the internal case — the tier comparison, the platform capabilities, the scarcity numbers. The window on founding sponsor status is real, not a sales tactic — these are hard slot limits.
**REDIRECT:** What would make the internal conversation easier — a one-pager with the tier breakdown, or a short call with your decision-maker included?

**RESPONSE:** No one can guarantee ROI — anyone who does is selling you something different than what they can deliver. What I can tell you is exactly what's measurable: booth visits, content downloads, lead list contacts, email open rates. Featured and Title give you the data infrastructure to calculate your own ROI after the event. The inputs are controlled. The output depends on your follow-up.
**REDIRECT:** If we're talking Featured or Title — how many qualified leads from the valuation space would you need from this event to call it a win?

**OBJECTION:** "Your pricing is too high / I can only do Core right now."
**RESPONSE:** Core is a real entry point, not a consolation prize. Twenty slots, your brand on every email and in the Expo Hall — if your goal is brand presence in the profession, that's what Core delivers at $1,000. The question is whether visibility alone matches your goals, or whether you need pipeline tools.

## TALK TRACK — Master Deck
*For use by: Eunice Lopez / Hansel Dobbs*

**OPENING (Slides 1–2):**
Open with the industry context, not the pitch. The valuation profession is at an inflection point — AI, regulatory change, market pressure — and appraisers don't have a national platform to address it. ValuSignal 2026 is that platform. You're not inviting them to advertise; you're inviting them to be part of the answer the industry is looking for.

**CONTEXT SETTING (Slides 3–5):**
Address the virtual objection early and proactively — don't wait for them to raise it. Virtual means reach (all 50 states), measurability (every interaction tracked), and no geographic barrier. Then hit the Pioneer angle hard: there's no prior event to benchmark against because this has never been done. That's the opportunity, not the risk.

**THE OFFER (Slides 6–11):**
Transition from platform capabilities to what the sponsor actually controls. The Expo Hall booth is a sales tool, not a banner. Lead data is exportable. The speaking slot is live in front of the full audience. Present the tier comparison side by side — let them self-select based on their goals before you push a recommendation. Only recommend a tier after they've told you what they're trying to accomplish.

**HANDLING SILENCE / OBJECTIONS:**
If the prospect goes quiet after the pricing slide, ask: "What's the main thing you'd want to walk away from ValuSignal with — awareness, or pipeline?" That question surfaces the right tier and moves the conversation forward. Don't fill silence with justification. Let them respond.

**THE CLOSE (Final slides):**
"The founding sponsor slots are hard limits — 20 for Core, 10 for Featured, 1 for Title. Once they're gone, the next company joins an established event. The companies in this conversation right now are the ones who get to be part of building it. The next step is a 20-minute call to confirm your tier and start onboarding. When works for you this week?"
