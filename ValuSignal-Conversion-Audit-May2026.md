# ValuSignal 2026 — Conversion Audit
**Prepared by:** Eunice Lopez, Operations
**Date:** May 23, 2026
**Presentation:** Internal Team — Tuesday, May 26, 2026
**Audience:** Client, Speaker Relations Coordinator, Marketing Manager, Email Team

---

## Executive Summary

ValuSignal 2026 has strong bones: a clear value proposition, a credible speaker lineup, real urgency drivers (UAD 3.6 mandate on November 2), and an active cold email campaign generating genuine interest. The infrastructure — Stripe, Make.com, Pipedrive, Instantly — is largely in place and functional.

The conversion gap is not a traffic problem or a product problem. It is a **messaging alignment problem**: the website, the email campaigns, and the LinkedIn presence are not telling the same story at the same moment in the buyer journey. Leads arrive at different points, receive different signals about what ValuSignal is and what it costs, and leave without a clear next step.

The good news: all of these gaps are fixable before June 15. This audit prioritizes changes by tier so the team can work in parallel without stepping on each other.

**Current conversion snapshot (Campaign 1 / Instantly):**
- 102 leads contacted → 5 registered = **4.9% conversion**
- 14 leads replied with interest but have not converted
- ~20 leads added in the last 10 days with no follow-up status yet
- Early bird deadline: **June 15, 2026** ($197 → $297)

## 1. Website Audit — valusignal.com

### What Is Working
- **Hero headline is strong.** "Where Valuation Does Business." is specific, confident, and differentiates from CE-credit conferences. Keep it.
- **Refund guarantee is present** on the registration page. This removes purchase risk and should be surfaced earlier.
- **Countdown timer** creates time awareness. Functional and visible.
- **$2.50/hour value framing** ("80 hours of content") is a smart anchor. It does the math the buyer won't do themselves.

### Opportunities for Improvement

**CRITICAL — Agent Pricing Lab is still live on the website.**
The Agent Pricing Lab was eliminated on April 21. As of today (May 23), the APL ticket ($97 early bird / $147 regular) is still listed on the registration page, the navigation bar still includes "Agent Pricing Lab," and the schedule page references an APL session. A prospective buyer who clicks that track, reads the description, and tries to register is being shown a product that no longer exists in its original form. This needs to be removed from navigation, registration, and schedule immediately — before any campaign drives traffic to the site.

**CRITICAL — Registration page says "Registration Opening Soon."**
The registration page shows ticket prices, an early bird deadline of June 15, and a "Register for 2026" CTA throughout the site — but the registration page itself displays "Registration Opening Soon. Enter your email to stay in the loop." If the Stripe checkout is live and accepting payments, this copy needs to be updated to reflect that immediately. If registration is not yet live, all email campaigns and LinkedIn posts should not be pointing to valusignal.com/register as a purchase destination.

**MEDIUM — Early bird deadline is only visible on the registration page.**
The homepage has a countdown timer (to the event) but no visible "$197 early bird ends June 15" messaging. A lead who visits the homepage and does not click through to register never sees the price urgency. The deadline should appear on the homepage, ideally near the pricing section and the CTA buttons.

**MEDIUM — No social proof above the fold.**
The homepage lists speakers in a carousel lower on the page, but the hero section makes no reference to the caliber of the lineup. For appraisers evaluating whether this event is worth $197, credibility markers (speaker affiliations, institutional names like Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac, Malinda Griffin as President of NAA, Joshua Walitt) should appear earlier — ideally within the first scroll.

**MEDIUM — Navigation still includes "Agent Pricing Lab" as a track.**
Beyond the registration page, the nav bar itself advertises a track that no longer exists. This creates confusion for any appraiser who explores the site before registering.

**SUGGESTED — The guarantee copy is buried.**
"If the conference doesn't deliver, we'll refund your ticket." is on the registration page. This sentence removes the single biggest objection for a $197 purchase. It should appear on the homepage near the pricing CTA, not just on the checkout page.

**SUGGESTED — "Subscribe for updates" on the schedule section implies the event is still being built.**
The schedule page has a "subscribe for updates" module that signals the program is incomplete. While some sessions may still be in development, the visible message to a potential buyer is "I don't know what I'm buying yet." Consider replacing this with a "Full schedule releasing [date]" message that sounds intentional rather than uncertain.

## 2. Email Campaign Audit

### Campaign 1 — Appraiser Cold Outreach (Instantly — ACTIVE)

**What Is Working**
- Sequence structure is solid: 1 initial + 5 follow-ups with A/B/C variants is the right architecture.
- The PDF offer ("Can I send the 1-page overview?") is a low-friction, curiosity-based CTA that generates replies without demanding a click to a sales page.
- Subject lines are short and personalized. Follow-up subjects are blank (same thread) — correct for deliverability.
- The speaker roster in follow-ups (Joshua Walitt, Jake Lew, Roy Meyer, Malinda Griffin, Michelle Czekalski Bradley) adds genuine institutional credibility.

**Conversion Data**
- 14 leads replied "interested" and were sent the PDF overview but have not registered.
- This is the warmest segment in the entire pipeline. 14 people said yes to the PDF and then went quiet.
- The interested lead follow-up sub-sequence (4 emails × 3 variants) needs to be running for all 14 of these contacts. If it is already running, confirm that none of these 14 have lapsed past the sequence window without conversion.

**Opportunities for Improvement**

*CRITICAL — Verify all 14 "interested/replied" leads are in the sub-sequence.*
The sub-sequence that follows a PDF send is the most conversion-critical touchpoint in the entire campaign. If any of these leads received the PDF but did not get the 4-email follow-up, they are warm leads going cold. Audit each of the 14 rows in the tracking sheet this week.

*CRITICAL — Early bird deadline must be June 15 in all active emails.*
The original early bird deadline was June 1. The extension to June 15 was confirmed. Verify that every active email in the Instantly sequence (including follow-ups already in progress) references June 15, not June 1. A lead receiving FU4 or FU5 this week might still be reading the wrong deadline.

*MEDIUM — ~20 leads added May 13–23 have no outreach status.*
These are the most recently added contacts. Some may still be in the early steps of the sequence (which is expected), but the blank status column means there is no visibility into where they are. The tracking sheet should be updated to reflect "In Sequence / Step X" for active leads so the team can monitor velocity.

*MEDIUM — The 1-page PDF overview should reference June 15, not June 1.*
If the PDF being sent to interested leads still shows June 1 as the early bird deadline, every PDF sent is delivering incorrect urgency. Confirm and update the PDF.

*SUGGESTED — Consider a direct registration link in the sub-sequence.*
The main campaign withholds links (correct for deliverability in the cold sequence). But the sub-sequence targeting leads who already replied is a warm audience — these leads have raised their hand. A direct link to valusignal.com/register in sub-sequence Email 3 or 4 is appropriate and removes friction for the ready-to-buy segment.

**Assessment**
This campaign targets a different buyer type (university career centers, advisors) with a different CTA (flyer). The ask is low-friction and appropriate for the audience. The 4 initial + 4 follow-up structure is reasonable.

**Opportunities for Improvement**

*MEDIUM — No launch date set.*
Career Launchpad is $47 early bird. The university audience has longer decision cycles (they need to share with students, who then need to act). This campaign should launch as soon as possible to allow for the full sequence to run before June 15.

*MEDIUM — The flyer needs to be confirmed as updated with June 15 pricing.*
If the flyer still shows $47 as early bird without a deadline, or references June 1, it needs to be updated before distribution.

*SUGGESTED — No clear handoff from flyer interest to registration.*
The campaign drives to a flyer, but the flyer should include a direct URL (valusignal.com/register) and the early bird price/deadline. Make the path from "interested student" to "registered student" explicit on the asset itself.

## 3. LinkedIn Audit

### Company Page — linkedin.com/company/valusignal

**What Is Working**
- Posts are consistent and cover the right topics: UAD 3.6, speaker spotlights, tech demos, Career Launchpad, revenue diversification.
- The Revenue Resilience Scorecard is a strong lead magnet for organic reach.
- Post frequency appears active (multiple posts in the last 7 days).

**Opportunities for Improvement**

*CRITICAL — 89 followers is very low for the campaign goals.*
With 89 followers, organic reach on company page posts is functionally zero for people outside the existing network. The posts being created are good content, but they are reaching a very small audience. The company page should not be the primary conversion channel for this event — it is better used as a credibility layer (so that when someone gets a cold email and Googles "ValuSignal," the LinkedIn page confirms it is a real organization with active content). The team should not be measuring company page reach as a primary KPI right now.

*MEDIUM — No early bird deadline or price urgency in any post.*
The posts announce what is at the event but do not create urgency to register now. At least 2–3 posts between now and June 15 should include a direct "Early bird pricing ends June 15 — $197 today, $297 after" message with the register link.

*SUGGESTED — Speaker spotlight posts do not include a registration CTA.*
Each speaker spotlight builds credibility but ends without asking the reader to act. Adding one line at the end — "See [Speaker Name] live at ValuSignal, Sept 18–19. Early bird: $197 through June 15. [link]" — turns a brand post into a conversion post without changing its character.

---

### Personal Account — linkedin.com/in/eunice-lopez-suarez

**Context**
Eunice posts daily, telling the story of the event from her perspective as an organizer. This is the most human and authentic content in the entire ValuSignal content ecosystem. The personal account functions as a trust-building channel — it shows the behind-the-scenes effort, the team, the decision-making, and the stakes.

**Opportunities for Improvement**

*MEDIUM — Story posts need an occasional bridge to action.*
Daily narrative posts build an audience that follows the journey. The gap is that readers who are interested in the event but not yet in any email sequence have no clear path from "I follow Eunice's posts" to "I registered." Roughly 1 in 5 posts should include a soft CTA: "If you're an appraiser curious about what we're building, the early bird window is open until June 15 — link in bio" or equivalent. The other 4 posts stay pure story.

*MEDIUM — The personal account likely has more reach than the company page.*
If Eunice's personal network is larger than 89 connections in the appraisal space, her posts are reaching more of the target audience than the company page. Content that lives only on the company page (speaker spotlights, track announcements) should be reshared or recreated for the personal account.

*SUGGESTED — Tag speakers in personal posts when relevant.*
When posting about a speaker or session, tagging that speaker (e.g., @JoshuaWalitt) can expand reach to their network, which is pre-qualified: people who follow a valuation professional are likely in valuation.
