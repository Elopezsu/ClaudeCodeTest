## PART 1 — Sponsor Deck Audit: What's Working and What to Fix

### What Is Working — Keep It

**The "Founding Sponsor" positioning is the strongest argument in the deck.**
"You're Not Buying a Slot. You're Buying a Founding Position" — with the supporting logic (no incumbent, first mover becomes the reference, Year 1 pricing won't return) — is more compelling than anything ROI-based. This framing is the reason a company says yes to a first-year event. Keep it in every deck, don't soften it.

**The tier differentiation language is clear and specific.**
- Core = brand presence ("companies testing brand recognition")
- Featured = pipeline ("companies that need contacts in their CRM, not just impressions")
- Title = partnership ("exclusive, co-branded, keynote-level")
This is the right frame. The prospect self-selects their tier based on what they need.

**The Zoom Events booth explanation (Featured/Title) is one of the strongest slides.**
"Your Booth Is a Sales Tool, Not a Banner" — with the specific breakdown of branded video, live rep chat, lead capture auto-logged, full analytics — answers the virtual skepticism objection before it's raised. Keep it exactly as is.

**The "From Yes to Live" process slide removes friction.**
Agreement → Invoice → Onboarding → Go Live → Event, with "from agreement to live on the website is typically under one week" — this tells the prospect the commitment is low-effort on their end. Keep it.

**The 4-audience breakdown (40/25/20/15) is credible.**
Practicing Appraisers (40%), Firm Owners & Principals (25%), Valuation Tech Adopters (20%), Adjacent Ecosystem (15%) — this tells a sponsor exactly who will see their brand and in what proportion.

### What to Fix — Deck Improvements

**FIX 1 (Critical): The ROI math uses 2,000 attendees as the base — but the current target is 300–600.**

The Core deck slide reads: *"2,000 attendees × $1,000 = $0.50 per impression."*

If a prospect asks "how many attendees do you have right now?" or "how many are you expecting?", this creates a credibility problem. The deck targets 300–600 focused professionals but the math assumes 2,000.

**Fix:** Change the ROI slide to use 500 as the conservative base and 1,000 as the optimistic base:
- *"500 attendees (conservative) × $1,000 = $2.00 per impression"*
- *"1,000 attendees × $1,000 = $1.00 per impression"*

Add context: *"The largest dedicated valuation conference to date drew ~600. Our target at launch is 300–600 focused professionals — no dilution from adjacent industries."*

This is more honest and still compelling. The quality-of-audience argument (100% valuation professionals vs. a mixed-industry event) is stronger than an inflated impression count anyway.

---

**FIX 2 (Medium): The Core deck has an inconsistency between the "4 placements" slide and the "6 touchpoints" slide.**

The benefits page shows **4** placements: Website Listing, Expo Hall Presence, Email Visibility, Social Recognition.
The "Your Logo Appears Every Time" slide lists **6** touchpoints: ValuSignal.com, Expo Hall Listing, Pre-Event Emails, Event-Day Comms, Post-Event Recap, Social Posts.

These are the same benefits but counted differently. A detail-oriented prospect notices this. Align the language: either consolidate "Pre-Event + Event-Day + Post-Event" into one "All Event Emails" line, or split them consistently across both slides.

---

**FIX 3 (Medium): The Title deck needs a stronger scarcity narrative on the opening slide.**

The Core deck opens with "20 slots available" — visible, specific, creates urgency.
The comparison table shows "1 slot" for Title — but this is buried in a table row.

A $10,000 decision needs the scarcity front and center. The Title deck cover or second slide should state: *"1 Slot. Once It's Gone, It's Gone."* with the same "Founding Sponsor" framing as the other decks. Right now, the Title deck relies on the prospect to notice "1 slot" in the comparison table.

---

**FIX 4 (Suggested): The Featured deck's 5-minute speaking slot needs more specificity.**

Current copy: *"Live or Simulive format. Your content, your CTA, delivered to the full attendee audience during a main session."*

A prospect negotiating Featured ($2,500) will want to know: During which session? How many attendees are in that session? Is it a dedicated slot or between speakers? Where does it appear in the run-of-show?

Add one sentence: *"Scheduled during a main-stage session between speakers — confirmed placement in the run-of-show at signing, not assigned day-of."*

---

**FIX 5 (Suggested): Add a traction slide to the Main Deck for outreach use.**

When sending the Main Deck cold to a prospect, the absence of any "here's where we are right now" data is noticeable for a first-year event. Add one slide:

**"Where ValuSignal Stands Today"**
- Confirmed speakers: 70+
- Association partners actively promoting: 3 (Appraisers on Purpose, Texas Residential Valuation Society, American Valuation Society)
- Cold outreach campaign: active in [X] states
- Early bird registrations: open through June 15
- Platform: Zoom Events (largest virtual events infrastructure)

This slide turns "we're a first-year event" from a risk into a proof-of-execution story.
